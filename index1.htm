Claude Monet

Claude Monet is one of the most famous Impressionist even to this day. One of his most famous peices, Water Lilies and the Japanese Bridge, depicts a serene pond filled with lilies and beautiful lush greenery behind a bridge. Monet became known for his very Impressionistic style. He developed a very unique style by repainting paintings he had already made over and over. Another of his peices is Argenteuil. This peice is somewhat different from his other works. It is a sailboat floating by a large bridge on a bright, sunny day. Monet helped to spead the word about impressionism through his beautiful and stylistic art.

Vincent Van Gogh

Vincent Van Gogh is one of the most recognizeable artist in the art world. His masterfull art showed a new approach to Impressionism. He made famous peices such as Starry Night, Self portrait with Bandaged ear, and Irises. The intresting thing about some of Van Gogh's art is that the are stories behind these works of art. For instance, Van Gogh's Self Portrait with Bandaged ear, was painted after he suffered a mental breakdown and severed his left ear. Although he struggle with a hard life his works of art are still admired today.

Paul Cezanne

Paul Cezanne, a french Impressionist, liked to observe nature to create his paintings. He strived his entire life to find a way to truly represent what he are observed on a canvas. He worked with binocular vision and single-point perspective to improve his art. He is know mostly for his beautifully rendered landscapes. One of his peices, The Bay of Marseilles, uses beautiful pastel colors to recreate a seaside village. Cezanne's art has been shown in galleries across the world and his art impacts other artists today.

Edouard Manet

Edouard Manet is known for his very loose brush strokes he used in his art. Manet's art created easily identifiable works using simple details and yet still integrating realism into his art. One of his major works, Luncheon on the grass, was very controversial. It highlighted two fully dressed men and two nude women. It was so controversial that the only art salon that would take his art was the Salon Des Refuses, or Salon of the Rejected. Although he had some controrvesial art, his style and use of realism is truly extrordinary.

Edgar Degas

Edgar Degas, most famous for his dancers, had a wide array of subjects. After his increasingly popular dancers, He began to depict more dancers backstage or in rehersal to emphasize their status as professionals. Degas was interested with portraiture and studied how a person's posture, dress, etc. could be related to their social status or job. Degas art has been featured and loved all around the world.
